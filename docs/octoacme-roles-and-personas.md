# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers ensure that features, products, and services are user-centric, intuitive, and accessible. They own the user experience, design systems, and usability best practices throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design mockups for new features
- Conduct usability testing and gather user feedback
- Collaborate with Product Managers on user flows and journeys
- Review and sign off on feature implementations for usability compliance
- Maintain and evolve design systems and component libraries
- Advocate for accessibility and inclusive design practices

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, delightful user experiences
- Reduce user friction and support costs
- Ensure consistent design patterns across products

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Developers and Product Managers
- Usability testing reports and findings
- Design system documentation and component specs

### Key Handoffs
- **With Product Managers**: Align on user stories, user research insights, and prioritization
- **With Developers**: Provide design specs, accessibility guidelines, and design tokens
- **With QA/Testing**: Define acceptance criteria for visual and interaction quality

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers plan, coordinate, and execute releases to production. They ensure that all release artifacts are ready, communication plans are in place, and deployment windows are executed smoothly with minimal risk.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain release schedules and roadmaps
- Prepare release checklists and deployment runbooks
- Coordinate with Developers, QA, and Product Managers on release readiness
- Manage communications with stakeholders and support teams
- Execute or oversee deployments and post-release verifications
- Document rollback procedures and incident playbooks
- Drive post-release reviews and retrospectives

### Goals
- Execute releases with zero or minimal downtime
- Reduce deployment risk and incident response time
- Improve release predictability and team confidence

### Typical Communication
- Release planning meetings and status updates
- Deployment window coordination and incident updates
- Post-release reviews and lessons learned

### Key Handoffs
- **With Project Managers**: Align on release timelines and scope
- **With Developers**: Collect build artifacts, deployment instructions, and migration steps
- **With QA/Testing**: Verify smoke tests and acceptance criteria before production deployment
- **With Support/Operations**: Brief on new features, known issues, and support escalation paths

---

## Customer Support Lead

### Role Summary
Customer Support Leads act as the voice of the customer and bridge between the delivery team and end users. They gather feedback on new features, troubleshoot issues, and ensure that customer needs drive product improvements.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and synthesize customer feedback on new features and releases
- Triage and escalate critical customer issues to engineering and product teams
- Create and maintain support documentation and FAQs for new features
- Coordinate with Product Managers on product roadmap and customer pain points
- Monitor customer satisfaction and sentiment metrics
- Participate in retrospectives to inform product and process improvements
- Train support team on new features and edge cases

### Goals
- Maximize customer satisfaction and retention
- Identify patterns and trends in customer feedback
- Reduce support ticket volume through better documentation and product design

### Typical Communication
- Weekly customer feedback summaries for Product and Engineering
- Feature release documentation and training materials
- Incident and escalation communications with delivery teams
- Monthly customer sentiment reports

### Key Handoffs
- **With Product Managers**: Share customer insights, feature requests, and pain points to inform prioritization
- **With Developers**: Report bugs, provide reproduction steps, and validate fixes
- **With Release Managers**: Coordinate on communication plans and support documentation for releases
- **With Project Managers**: Highlight customer-impacting risks and dependencies
